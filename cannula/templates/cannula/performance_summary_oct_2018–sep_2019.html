Perinatal death mortality remained stagnant over the four FY19 quarters, with stillbirths being the largest contributors - hence the need to strengthen processes of care around the Antenatal, Labor and Delivery. Over a period of 6 months (April – September 2019) data of the BABIES matrix shows that 44.5% (204) of the 458 perinatal deaths were fresh still births (FSBs) while macerated stillbirths (MSB) and early neonatal deaths contributed 36.9% and 18.6% respectively. MSBs and FSBs were highest in in >2,500g weight babies while early neonatal deaths were highest in the 1,500 – 2,499g weight babies. High still birth rate among >2500g point to the gaps in the quality of care during late pregnancy and labour and delivery process that the women who delivered within the April – Sep 2019 period while the high early neonatal deaths within the >2,499g babies point to gaps in essential newborn care, resuscitation newborn and management of the sick newborn. In line with the labour process, 75% (14,883/19,855) of the deliveries conducted were monitored using a partograph while 69% (1,519/2,208) of the low birth weight babies received Kangaroo mother care (KMC) for warmth. We will continue using the BABIES matrix to appreciate type of Perinatal deaths and focus our interventions to specific time-periods along the ANC-Labor-Delivery continuum.
